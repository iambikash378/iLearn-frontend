Text Preprocessing

Text preprocessing is a crucial step in natural language processing (NLP) that prepares raw text data for analysis. It involves several steps to clean, normalize, and structure the text.

Common preprocessing tasks include:

  • Lowercasing: Converting all text to lowercase for uniformity.
  • Removing punctuation: Cleaning symbols that donâ€™t add meaning.
  • Tokenization: Splitting text into words or phrases.
  • Stopword removal: Removing common words like "the", "and", "is".
  • Stemming/Lemmatization: Reducing words to their base form.

Proper text preprocessing improves model accuracy and efficiency in NLP applications like sentiment analysis, text classification, and chatbots.
